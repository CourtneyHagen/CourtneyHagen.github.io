Management

Customer Service Manager - Walmart Canada

I was promoted from a Cashier to a Cusomter Service Manager shortly after graduating SMU. This was my second management position, as my previous job had been as a shift manager. This was a junior management position. My job was essentially to manage the Customer Experience Front End department. Aside from hiring, promoting, firing, and regular scheduling, I was responsible for all things unless escalation was required. On a day to day basis, I would be given a Team Map of my staff for the day and would be responsible for making sure they got their breaks and to keep track of call-ins and no-shows to make sure we were meeting staff demand. We also had goals for our staff and it was my job to verify those goals were being met and act accordingly if they weren't. My management style was a sympathetic one, as I knew just how stressful their jobs were, and I knew that if my staff did not feel appreciated I would lose them or they would under perform. I did my best to make sure I was not making them feel inferior and to be as lenient and tolerant as possible, however, I was firm when I felt this was being taken advantage of. I also did not tolerate disrespect to me or my staff from anyone.

As there was normally more than one CSM, if I was not in charge of the Team Map that day, my primary responsibility would be managing lines. This meant directing customers to faster cashiers or shorter lines, and calling up cash-trained staff or the manager on duty if the lines were too long. Essentially, the role was the juggling of humans in a way that made the store the most efficient possible. Of course, this was only half of the role.

The other half of the role was focused on resolving customer issues. It was a highly involved problem solving job. If you go to the customer service desk and ask to speak to a manager, it was probably someone like me you would speak to first. I have dealt with anything from stealing all the way to customers assaulting each other. I have been responsible for resolving issues requiring me to not only interpret store policies, but to interpret laws and asssert my decisions based on them. For example, common issues included, is this customer money laundering or stealing someone's identity for a credit card? I enjoyed the job because both the problem solving process and execution were fun to me. I enjoyed having a job where the entire day I was kept on my toes and exposed to new problem solving opportunities. I will carry the skills I learned from this position through all of my future jobs.
